Early blight disease is a common fungal disease that affects tomato plants. It usually starts as small brown spots on the lower leaves of the plant and can spread to the upper leaves if left untreated. Fortunately, there are several remedies that you can try to control early blight disease in your tomato plants:

2: Mulch: Mulching around the base of the tomato plants can help prevent the soil from splashing onto the leaves and spreading the fungal spores. You can use a layer of straw, grass clippings, or shredded leaves for mulching.
3: Water at the base: Watering your tomato plants at the base instead of from above can help prevent the spread of the fungal spores. This will also help prevent the leaves from getting wet, which can promote the growth of the fungus.
